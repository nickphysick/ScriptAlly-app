Three tiers of chrome

Every route belongs to exactly one tier, and each tier owns its navigation. Moving between tiers is always a deliberate step — into the workshop, out to the desk, or back to the shop window.

Tier 1

Marketing

Chrome: pinned marketing nav · auth-aware right side
/ — landing/pricing — public pricing
Everyone lands here, always. Logged out: Log in + Start tracking. Logged in: avatar + Open dashboard — no auto-redirect away from /. The pinned nav grows its grey hairline on scroll.
Tier 2

Focus

Chrome: slim top bar · no rail
/account/plans — manage subscription/help
Step-out-of-the-workshop pages. Wordmark (→ /), mono breadcrumb, ← Back to dashboard, avatar. Settings and money decisions get quiet, not navigation.
Tier 3

Workspace

Chrome: AppShell rail · unchanged
/dashboard/queries · ?q= /agents · /discover/manuscripts · /packages /import
The workshop. Rail, themes, timeline drawer — exactly as built. Nothing in this project touches workspace chrome.

The journeys between them

/ → signup → /dashboardGet started completes into the workspace; first-run onboarding untouched.
/ (logged in)Stays on the landing; "Open dashboard" is the door. One canonical front page for everyone.
rail avatar/gear → /accountRail disappears; focus bar appears. "← Back to dashboard" returns to the workspace.
in-app upgrade CTAs → /plansSubscription management lives in focus chrome; the public /pricing stays marketing.
focus wordmark → /The wordmark always goes to the front door, from any tier.
#/login · #/signinPre-auth hashes keep working; login is reachable from the marketing nav.
