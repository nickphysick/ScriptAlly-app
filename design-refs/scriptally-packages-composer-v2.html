Packages display · Composer v2

Top: three treatments for the packages screen. Bottom: the composer scaled up, with "Copy an existing package" retired from the strip and reborn as a quiet header control opening a right-hand drawer.
1 · List + reading pane (my lean)
The Queries Hub grammar, reused: compact package list left (name, three type chips showing filled/empty at a glance, one stat line), full reading pane right for the selected package — each material as a roomy row with pill, title and filename, big stat figures along the foot, actions in the pane header. Nothing is cramped because nothing has to fit in a card; and it scales to twenty packages without layout change. The dotted ghost "New" lives at the list foot.

2 · Roomy cards
Keeps the grid, fixes the cramp: two columns max, each material gets its own spaced row (icon tile, Playfair title, filename beneath in mono — two lines, not one squeezed line), hairlines between rows, actions up in the band header, and the stats moved into a tinted footer strip so they stop competing with the contents. Half the density, twice the legibility, no new navigation model.

3 · The ledger
Packages as full-width rows in one bordered register: name, then the contents as bordered chips (dashed = empty slot), stats right-aligned. Extremely scannable for comparing what differs between packages — the chips line up vertically — and the most compact. Weakest at showing filenames (they'd live in a hover or the row's expanded state).

Composer v2 — bigger, with the copy drawer
The fork strip is gone. In its place: a muted "⧉ Copy existing" control in the band's top right, which slides open a right-hand drawer of your packages — each a mini card with contents and a soft-pink "⧉ Copy into composer" — closing on choice or ✕. Everything is scaled up: 23px name input, 72px manifest rows with 16.5px titles, 17px picker header, 16px picker cards, roomier padding throughout. The drawer is open in this sketch; click ✕ / ⧉ Copy existing to toggle it.
